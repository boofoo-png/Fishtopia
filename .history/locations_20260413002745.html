Locations

Purradise Island is made up of different areas that support the story, fishing progression, and daily life of the Kittezens. Some locations help move the game forward directly, while others add more personality and world-building to the island.

Town Locations

These locations help show how the island functions as a close-knit town. Some support progression directly, while others build atmosphere and relationships.

Town Hall

Town Hall is where Mayor Whiskers tracks Finn’s progress and grants permits to unlock new fishing areas. It is one of the most important locations for story progression because new parts of the map are tied to the mayor and your fishing milestones.

Players can access it in the main town area early in the game.

General Store

The general store gives players access to supplies like bait and other useful fishing items. As the game progresses, better bait becomes important for reaching certain fish and locations.

Players can visit the general store once they start exploring the main town.

Saloon

The saloon is more of a social and relaxing space on the island. It does not really drive progression, but it adds personality and whimsy to the town and makes the world feel more lived in.

Kittezens can stop here for silly drinks like tuna juice and milk.

School

The school is associated with Professor Clive, who teaches players more about fish species and helps document discoveries. This location adds world building and supports the fish collection side of the game.

It is available in town and helps make the island feel like a real community.

Dock

The dock is important because it connects to Finn’s arrival on the island and their original goal of repairing the boat and leaving.

Even later in the game, it still acts as a reminder of Finn’s possible departure.

Fishing Areas

These areas shape the game’s progression. Each new location introduces stronger fish, new challenges, and more of the island’s mystery.

Town Pond

Town Pond is one of the starter fishing areas. It is meant to ease players into the fishing loop with more common fish and lower difficulty.

This area is available early and helps players learn how to catch fish, sell them, and begin earning clams.

Beachside

Beachside is separate from the pond and focuses more on ocean and salt-water catches. It expands the player’s options early and helps make the island feel geographically varied.

This area becomes especially useful once salt water bait and better tools come into play.

Cave Pond

Cave Pond is a darker and more difficult fishing area with uncommon fish. It introduces more tension and starts pushing the player into the next level of progression.

Players unlock this area later through progress, equipment upgrades, and the mayor’s permit system.

Magical Forest Lake

Magical Forest Lake introduces rarer and more unusual fish. It adds a brighter, more enchanted feeling to the game world and makes exploration feel more rewarding.

By this point, the player is deeper into both the game’s mechanics and its story.

Volcano Lake

Volcano Lake is the final major fishing area and the highest challenge in the game. It is tied to legendary fish and marks the end stage of area progression.

Reaching this location shows how far the player has come in both gameplay and story progression.

Special Locations

These locations matter more to the emotional side of the game. They help shape Finn’s connection to the island and to their grandfather’s past.

Grandfather’s House

This house becomes Finn’s home base after arriving on the island. It is where the inherited fishing business begins and where parts of Finn’s grandfather’s history can be uncovered.

It acts as both a practical location and an emotional anchor for the story.

Storm Shoreline

The shoreline is where Finn washes up after the storm damages the boat. It marks the start of the game and the moment Finn’s temporary visit becomes something much more complicated.

This location helps establish the emotional tone of the story from the very beginning.
